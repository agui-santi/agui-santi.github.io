About

I'm a KICP Fellow at the Kavli Institute for Cosmological Physics at the University of Chicago. I completed my PhD in cosmology at the University of Cambridge, supervised by Enrico Pajer and Scott Melville. I started out studying Physics at the Universidad Autónoma de Madrid (UAM), spent my final undergraduate year on exchange at Imperial College London, and completed Part III in Cambridge during the 2020–21 academic year before staying on for my PhD.

My research sits at the intersection of theory and observation: I study how the universe's largest structures encode signatures of its earliest moments, from primordial non-Gaussianity in inflation to the imprint of dark matter decay on cosmic structure, combining open effective field theory techniques with large survey datasets like DESI.

Research Projects

Primordial Non-Gaussianity Constraints on Dissipative Inflation

Decaying Dark Matter Cosmology

Modeling the impact of dark matter decay on structure formation and late-time cosmological observables.

DESI BAO Analysis

Analyzing baryon acoustic oscillation signals from DESI survey data to refine cosmological parameter estimates.
